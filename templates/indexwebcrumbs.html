Project Overview

Heart disease is the leading cause of death globally. This project aims to predict the likelihood of heart disease based on various health parameters using machine learning techniques. Early detection can significantly improve treatment outcomes and save lives.

Our model has been trained on a comprehensive dataset containing patient data such as age, sex, blood pressure, cholesterol levels, and other critical health metrics. The analysis provides insights into the most significant factors contributing to heart disease.

Key Insights

Demographics

Males are more susceptible to heart disease than females, with a ratio of approximately 65:35. The risk increases significantly after the age of 50.

Risk Factors

High cholesterol and blood pressure are strongly correlated with heart disease. Patients with these conditions should be monitored more closely.

Lifestyle Impact

Regular exercise and controlled diet significantly reduce the risk of heart disease across all age groups and genders.

Predictive Factors

Age, cholesterol levels, and blood pressure are the most significant predictors in our model, accounting for over 70% of the prediction accuracy.
